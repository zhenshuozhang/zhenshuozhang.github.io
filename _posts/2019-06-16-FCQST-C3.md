#### 3.2 Quantum Bits

- Dirac notation

  - $|\psi\rangle$
  - linear

- noiseless qubit: $|\psi\rangle  \equiv \alpha|0\rangle+\beta|1\rangle$

  - $|\alpha|^2+|\beta|^2=1$
  - probability amplitudes

- superposition states

  - not Boolean algebra for superposition states
  - kets: $|0\rangle\equiv[1,0]^T$ , $|1\rangle\equiv[0,1]^T$
  - bras: $\langle0|rangle\equiv[1,0]$ , $\langle1|\equiv[0,1]$
  - $\langle0|=(|0\rangle)^T$ $\langle1|=(|1\rangle)^T$
  - $|\psi\rangle  =\alpha|0\rangle+\beta|1\rangle=[\alpha,\beta]^T$

- qubit

- equivalent state

  - $|\psi \rangle \equiv r_0|0\rangle + r_1 e^{i(\varphi_1+\varphi_0)}|1\rangle$

- Bloch sphere representation

  - $|\psi \rangle \equiv cos(\theta/2)|0\rangle + sin(\theta/2)e^{i\varphi}|1\rangle$

- calculate: state is $|0\rangle$

  - $$
    \begin{align}
    \lang0||\psi\rangle&=\lang0|(\alpha|0\rangle+\beta|1\rangle)\\
    &=\alpha\lang0||0\rangle+\beta\lang0||1\rangle\\
    &=\alpha\begin{bmatrix}1&0\end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix}1\\0\end{bmatrix}+\beta\begin{bmatrix}1&0\end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix}0\\1\end{bmatrix}\\
    &=\alpha*1+\beta*0\\
    &=\alpha
    \end{align}
    $$

  - braket: $\langle0|\psi\rangle\equiv\langle0||\psi\rangle$, means the probability amplitude for state $|0\rangle$

- Euclidean norm

  - $\||\psi\rangle\|_2\equiv\sqrt{\langle\psi|\psi\rangle}=1$

- basis

  - computational basis

    - $|0\rangle$ $|1\rangle$

  - "+/-", Hadamard, diagonal basis

    - $|+\rangle\equiv\frac{|0\rangle+|1\rangle}{\sqrt{2}}$ $|-\rangle\equiv\frac{|0\rangle-|1\rangle}{\sqrt{2}}$
    - orthonormal
    - important

  - $$
    \begin{align}
    \langle+|\psi\rangle&=\langle+|(\alpha|0\rangle+\beta|1\rangle)\\
    &=\alpha\langle+|0\rangle+\beta\langle+|1\rangle\\
    &=\frac{\alpha+\beta}{\sqrt{2}}
    \end{align}
    $$

    $$
    \langle-|\psi\rangle=\frac{\alpha-\beta}{\sqrt{2}}
    $$

  - $$
    |\psi\rangle=(\frac{\alpha+\beta}{\sqrt{2}})|+\rangle+(\frac{\alpha-\beta}{\sqrt{2}})|-\rangle
    $$

  - $$
    |\psi\rangle=(|+\rangle\langle+|+|-\rangle\langle-|)|\psi\rangle
    $$

    - because order does not matter

- identity operator

  - $I=|+\rangle\langle+|+|-\rangle\langle-|$
  - $I=|0\rangle\langle0|+|1\rangle\langle1|$

#### 3.3.2 Commutators and Anticommutators (交换子)

- commutator $[A,B]$ of 2 operators A and B 
  - $[A,B]\equiv AB-BA$
  - 2 operators commute if and only if their commutator is equal to 0 (对易)
- anticommutator $\{A,B\}$ of 2 operators A and B
  - $\{A,B\}\equiv AB+BA$
  - 2 operators anticommute if their commutator is equal to 0

    $$

#### 3.4.1 Probability, expectation, and variance of an operator

- Z operator
  - $Z=|0\rangle \langle0|-|1\rangle\langle1|$
  - Hermitian operator
    - $\prod_0\equiv|0\rangle\langle0|$
    - $\prod_1\equiv|1\rangle\langle1|$
    - projection operator because $\prod^2=\prod$
    - $Z=\prod_0-\prod_1$
- *von Neumann measurement*: for any orthonormal basis $\{|\phi_i\rangle\}_{i\in\{0,1\}}$, the measurement operators are $\{|\phi_i\rangle\langle\phi_i|\}_{i\in\{0,1\}}$, and the state reduces to $|\phi_i\rangle\langle\phi_i|\psi\rangle/|\langle\phi_i|\psi\rangle|$ with probability $\langle\psi|\phi_i\rangle\langle\phi_i|\psi\rangle=|\langle\phi_i|\psi\rangle|^2$.
- expectation
  - $\langle Z \rangle\equiv\langle\psi|Z|\psi\rangle$
- deviation
  - $\triangle Z\equiv\langle(Z-\langle Z\rangle )^2\rangle^{1/2}$
  - the uncertainty of the observable Z when the state is $|\psi\rangle$
- variance
  - $(\triangle Z)^2$

#### 3.4.2 The uncertainty principle

- $$
  \triangle Z\triangle X\ge\frac{1}{2}|\langle\psi|[Z,X]|\psi\rangle|
  $$

- define $Z_0\equiv Z-\langle Z\rangle$ , $X_0\equiv X-\langle X\rangle$
  $$
  \triangle Z\triangle X=\langle\psi|Z_0^2|\psi\rangle^{1/2}\langle\psi|X_0^2|\psi\rangle^{1/2}\ge|\langle\psi|Z_0X_0|\psi\rangle|
  $$

- $Re\{A\}\equiv(A+A^T)/2$ , $Im\{A\}\equiv(A-A^T)/2i$
  $$
  \begin{equation}
  Re\{Z_0X_0\}=\frac{Z_0X_0+X_0Z_0}{2}\equiv \frac{\{Z_0,X_0\}}{2}
  \end{equation}
  $$

  $$
  \begin{equation}
  Im\{Z_0X_0\}=\frac{Z_0X_0-X_0Z_0}{2i}\equiv \frac{[Z_0,X_0]}{2i}
  \end{equation}
  $$

- $$
  \begin{align}
  |\langle\psi|Z_0X_0|\psi\rangle|&=|\langle\psi|Re\{Z_0X_0\}|\psi\rangle+i\langle\psi|Im\{Z_0X_0\}|\psi\rangle|\\
  &\ge|\langle\psi|Im\{Z_0X_0\}|\psi\rangle|\\
  &=|\langle\psi|[Z_0,X_0]|\psi\rangle|/2\\
  &=|\langle\psi|[Z,X]|\psi\rangle|/2
  \end{align}
  $$

#### 3.5.4 The No-Cloning theorem

- impossible to build a *universal copier* of quantum states

  - a universal copier would be a device that could copy any arbitrary quantum state that is input to it

- proof

  - suppose operator $U$ acting as a universal copier

  - input $|\psi\rangle=\alpha |0\rangle+\beta|1\rangle$ as the first qubit and input an ancilla qubit $|0\rangle$ as the second qubit, such a device should "write" the first qubit to the second qubit slot as follows:
    $$
    \begin{align}
    U|\psi\rangle|0\rangle&=|\psi\rangle|\psi\rangle\\
    &=(\alpha|0\rangle+\beta|1\rangle)(\alpha|0\rangle+\beta|1\rangle)\\
    &=\alpha^2|0\rangle|0\rangle+\alpha\beta|0\rangle|1\rangle+\alpha\beta|1\rangle|0\rangle+\beta^2|1\rangle|1\rangle
    \end{align}
    $$

  - in particular, it also copies the states $|0\rangle$ and $|1\rangle$ as follows:
    $$
    U|0\rangle|0\rangle=|0\rangle|0\rangle,U|1\rangle|0\rangle=|1\rangle|1\rangle
    $$

  - imply on a superposition $\alpha|0\rangle+\beta|1\rangle$ as follows:
    $$
    U(\alpha|0\rangle+\beta|1\rangle)|0\rangle=\alpha|0\rangle|0\rangle+\beta|1\rangle|1\rangle
    $$

  - contradiction
    $$
    \exists\alpha,\beta:\alpha^2|0\rangle|0\rangle+\alpha\beta|0\rangle|1\rangle+\alpha\beta|1\rangle|0\rangle+\beta^2|1\rangle|1\rangle\neq\alpha|0\rangle|0\rangle+\beta|1\rangle|1\rangle
    $$

- another proof by exploiting unitarity of quantum evolutions

  - suppose operator $U$ acting as a universal copier

  - consider 2 arbitrary states $|\psi\rangle$ and $|\phi\rangle$
    $$
    U|\psi\rangle|0\rangle=|\psi\rangle|\psi\rangle,U|\phi\rangle|0\rangle=|\phi\rangle|\phi\rangle
    $$

  - consider the probability amplitude $\langle\psi|\langle\psi||\phi\rangle|\phi\rangle$
    $$
    \langle\psi|\langle\psi||\phi\rangle|\phi\rangle=\langle\psi|\phi\rangle\langle\psi|\phi\rangle=\langle\psi|\phi\rangle^2
    $$

  - using the property $U^TU=I$ :
    $$
    \begin{align}
    \langle\psi|\langle\psi||\phi\rangle|\phi\rangle&=\langle\psi|\lang0|U^TU|\phi\rangle|0\rangle\\
    &=\langle\psi|\lang0||\phi\rangle|0\rangle\\
    &=\langle\psi|\phi\rangle\lang0|0\rangle\\
    &=\langle\psi|\phi\rangle
    \end{align}
    $$

  - consequence:
    $$
    \langle\psi|\langle\psi||\phi\rangle|\phi\rangle=\langle\psi|\phi\rangle^2=\langle\psi|\phi\rangle
    $$
    only for $\langle\psi|\phi\rangle=1$ and $\langle\psi|\phi\rangle=0$ , means the two states are orthogonal to each other

- several applications

  - the security of the quantum key distribution protocol
  - application in quantum Shannon theory because we can use it to reason about the quantum capacity of a certain quantum channel known as the erasure channel.

### 3.7 Summary and Extensions to Qudit States

- qudit: on d-dimensional systems

#### 3.7.1 Qudits

- a qudit state $|\psi\rangle$ is an arbitrary superposition of some set of orthonormal basis states $\{|j\rangle\}_{j\in\{0,...,d-1\}}$ for a d-dimensional quantum syste
  $$
  |\psi\rangle\equiv\sum_{j=0}^{d-1}\alpha_j|j\rangle
  $$

- the amplitudes $\alpha_j$ obey the normalization condition $\sum_{j=0}^{d-1}|\alpha_j|^2=1$

#### 3.7.2 Unitary evolution

- cyclic shift operator $X(x)$:

  - $$
    X(x)|j\rangle=|x\oplus j\rangle
    $$

  - $\oplus$ is a cyclic addition operator, result is $(x+j) mod (d)$

  - $X(x)$ is a qudit generalization of the $X$ Pauli operator

#### 3.7.3 Measurement of qudits

- Hermitian operator $A$

  - $$
    A=\sum_jf(j)\Pi_j
    $$

  - $\Pi_j\Pi_k=\Pi_j\delta_{j,k}$ and $\sum_j\Pi_j=I$

  - A measurement of the operator $A$ then returns the result $j$ with the following probability
    $$
    p(j)=\langle\psi|\Pi_j|\psi\rangle
    $$

  - resulting state
    $$
    \frac{\Pi_j|\psi\rangle}{\sqrt{p(j)}}
    $$

  - expectation of operator $A$
    $$
    \mathbb{E}(A)=\sum_jf(j)\langle\psi|\Pi_j|\psi\rangle=\langle\psi|\sum_jf(j)\Pi_j|\psi\rangle=\langle\psi|A|\psi\rangle
    $$
