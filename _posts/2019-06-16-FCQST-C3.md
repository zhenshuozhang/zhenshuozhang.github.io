#### 3.3.2 Commutators and Anticommutators (交换子)

- commutator $[A,B]$ of 2 operators A and B 
  - $[A,B]\equiv AB-BA$
  - 2 operators commute if and only if their commutator is equal to 0 (对易)

#### 3.4.1 Probability, expectation, and variance of an operator

- Z operator
  - $Z=\vert0\rangle \langle0\vert-\vert1\rangle\langle1\vert$
  - Hermitian operator
    - $\prod_0\equiv\vert0\rangle\langle0\vert$
    - $\prod_1\equiv\vert1\rangle\langle1\vert$
    - projection operator because $\prod^2=\prod$
    - $Z=\prod_0-\prod_1$
- *von Neumann measurement*: for any orthonormal basis $\{\vert\phi_i\rangle\}_{i\in\{0,1\}}$, the measurement operators are $\{\vert\phi_i\rangle\langle\phi_i\vert\}_{i\in\{0,1\}}$, and the state reduces to $\vert\phi_i\rangle\langle\phi_i\vert\psi\rangle/\vert\langle\phi_i\vert\psi\rangle\vert$ with probability $\langle\psi\vert\phi_i\rangle\langle\phi_i\vert\psi\rangle=\vert\langle\phi_i\vert\psi\rangle\vert^2$.
- expectation
  - $\langle Z \rangle\equiv\langle\psi\vertZ\vert\psi\rangle$
- deviation
  - $\triangle Z\equiv\langle(Z-\langle Z\rangle )^2\rangle^{1/2}$
  - the uncertainty of the observable Z when the state is $\vert\psi\rangle$
- variance
  - $(\triangle Z)^2$

#### 3.4.2 The uncertainty principle

- $$
  \triangle Z\triangle X\ge\frac{1}{2}\vert\langle\psi\vert[Z,X]\vert\psi\rangle\vert
  $$

- define $Z_0\equiv Z-\langle Z\rangle$ , $X_0\equiv X-\langle X\rangle$
  $$
  \triangle Z\triangle X=\langle\psi\vertZ_0^2\vert\psi\rangle^{1/2}\langle\psi\vertX_0^2\vert\psi\rangle^{1/2}\ge\vert\langle\psi\vertZ_0X_0\vert\psi\rangle\vert
  $$

- $Re\{A\}\equiv(A+A^T)/2$ , $Im\{A\}\equiv(A-A^T)/2i$
  $$
  \begin{equation}
  Re\{Z_0X_0\}=\frac{Z_0X_0+X_0Z_0}{2}\equiv \frac{\{Z_0,X_0\}}{2}
  \end{equation}
  $$

  $$
  \begin{equation}
  Im\{Z_0X_0\}=\frac{Z_0X_0-X_0Z_0}{2i}\equiv \frac{[Z_0,X_0]}{2i}
  \end{equation}
  $$

- $$
  \begin{align}
  \vert\langle\psi\vertZ_0X_0\vert\psi\rangle\vert&=\vert\langle\psi\vertRe\{Z_0X_0\}\vert\psi\rangle+i\langle\psi\vertIm\{Z_0X_0\}\vert\psi\rangle\vert\\
  &\ge\vert\langle\psi\vertIm\{Z_0X_0\}\vert\psi\rangle\vert\\
  &=\vert\langle\psi\vert[Z_0,X_0]\vert\psi\rangle\vert/2\\
  &=\vert\langle\psi\vert[Z,X]\vert\psi\rangle\vert/2
  \end{align}
  $$

#### 3.5.4 The No-Cloning theorem

- impossible to build a *universal copier* of quantum states

  - a universal copier would be a device that could copy any arbitrary quantum state that is input to it

- proof

  - suppose operator $U$ acting as a universal copier

  - input $\vert\psi\rangle=\alpha \vert0\rangle+\beta\vert1\rangle$ as the first qubit and input an ancilla qubit $\vert0\rangle$ as the second qubit, such a device should "write" the first qubit to the second qubit slot as follows:
    $$
    \begin{align}
    U\vert\psi\rangle\vert0\rangle&=\vert\psi\rangle\vert\psi\rangle\\
    &=(\alpha\vert0\rangle+\beta\vert1\rangle)(\alpha\vert0\rangle+\beta\vert1\rangle)\\
    &=\alpha^2\vert0\rangle\vert0\rangle+\alpha\beta\vert0\rangle\vert1\rangle+\alpha\beta\vert1\rangle\vert0\rangle+\beta^2\vert1\rangle\vert1\rangle
    \end{align}
    $$

  - in particular, it also copies the states $\vert0\rangle$ and $\vert1\rangle$ as follows:
    $$
    U\vert0\rangle\vert0\rangle=\vert0\rangle\vert0\rangle,U\vert1\rangle\vert0\rangle=\vert1\rangle\vert1\rangle
    $$

  - imply on a superposition $\alpha\vert0\rangle+\beta\vert1\rangle$ as follows:
    $$
    U(\alpha\vert0\rangle+\beta\vert1\rangle)\vert0\rangle=\alpha\vert0\rangle\vert0\rangle+\beta\vert1\rangle\vert1\rangle
    $$

  - contradiction
    $$
    \exists\alpha,\beta:\alpha^2\vert0\rangle\vert0\rangle+\alpha\beta\vert0\rangle\vert1\rangle+\alpha\beta\vert1\rangle\vert0\rangle+\beta^2\vert1\rangle\vert1\rangle\neq\alpha\vert0\rangle\vert0\rangle+\beta\vert1\rangle\vert1\rangle
    $$

- another proof by exploiting unitarity of quantum evolutions

  - suppose operator $U$ acting as a universal copier

  - consider 2 arbitrary states $\vert\psi\rangle$ and $\vert\phi\rangle$
    $$
    U\vert\psi\rangle\vert0\rangle=\vert\psi\rangle\vert\psi\rangle,U\vert\phi\rangle\vert0\rangle=\vert\phi\rangle\vert\phi\rangle
    $$

  - consider the probability amplitude $\langle\psi\vert\langle\psi\vert\vert\phi\rangle\vert\phi\rangle$
    $$
    \langle\psi\vert\langle\psi\vert\vert\phi\rangle\vert\phi\rangle=\langle\psi\vert\phi\rangle\langle\psi\vert\phi\rangle=\langle\psi\vert\phi\rangle^2
    $$

  - using the property $U^TU=I$ :
    $$
    \begin{align}
    \langle\psi\vert\langle\psi\vert\vert\phi\rangle\vert\phi\rangle&=\langle\psi\vert\langle0\vertU^TU\vert\phi\rangle\vert0\rangle\\
    &=\langle\psi\vert\langle0\vert\vert\phi\rangle\vert0\rangle\\
    &=\langle\psi\vert\phi\rangle\langle0\vert0\rangle\\
    &=\langle\psi\vert\phi\rangle
    \end{align}
    $$

  - consequence:
    $$
    \langle\psi\vert\langle\psi\vert\vert\phi\rangle\vert\phi\rangle=\langle\psi\vert\phi\rangle^2=\langle\psi\vert\phi\rangle
    $$
    only for $\langle\psi\vert\phi\rangle=1$ and $\langle\psi\vert\phi\rangle=0$ , means the two states are orthogonal to each other

- several applications

  - the security of the quantum key distribution protocol
  - application in quantum Shannon theory because we can use it to reason about the quantum capacity of a certain quantum channel known as the erasure channel.

### 3.7 Summary and Extensions to Qudit States

- qudit: on d-dimensional systems

#### 3.7.1 Qudits

- a qudit state $\vert\psi\rangle$ is an arbitrary superposition of some set of orthonormal basis states $\{\vertj\rangle\}_{j\in\{0,...,d-1\}}$ for a d-dimensional quantum syste
  $$
  \vert\psi\rangle\equiv\sum_{j=0}^{d-1}\alpha_j\vertj\rangle
  $$

- the amplitudes $\alpha_j$ obey the normalization condition $\sum_{j=0}^{d-1}\vert\alpha_j\vert^2=1$

#### 3.7.2 Unitary evolution

- cyclic shift operator $X(x)$:

  - $$
    X(x)\vertj\rangle=\vertx\oplus j\rangle
    $$

  - $\oplus$ is a cyclic addition operator, result is $(x+j) mod (d)$

  - $X(x)$ is a qudit generalization of the $X$ Pauli operator

#### 3.7.3 Measurement of qudits

- Hermitian operator $A$

  - $$
    A=\sum_jf(j)\Pi_j
    $$

  - $\Pi_j\Pi_k=\Pi_j\delta_{j,k}$ and $\sum_j\Pi_j=I$

  - A measurement of the operator $A$ then returns the result $j$ with the following probability
    $$
    p(j)=\langle\psi\vert\Pi_j\vert\psi\rangle
    $$

  - resulting state
    $$
    \frac{\Pi_j\vert\psi\rangle}{\sqrt{p(j)}}
    $$

  - expectation of operator $A$
    $$
    \mathbb{E}(A)=\sum_jf(j)\langle\psi\vert\Pi_j\vert\psi\rangle=\langle\psi\vert\sum_jf(j)\Pi_j\vert\psi\rangle=\langle\psi\vertA\vert\psi\rangle
    $$
