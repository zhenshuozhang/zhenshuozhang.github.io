#### 3.3.2 Commutators and Anticommutators (交换子)

- commutator $[A,B]$ of 2 operators A and B 
  - $[A,B]\equiv AB-BA$
  - 2 operators commute if and only if their commutator is equal to 0 (对易)
- anticommutator $\{A,B\}$ of 2 operators A and B
  - $\{A,B\}\equiv AB+BA$
  - 2 operators anticommute if their commutator is equal to 0

#### 3.3.3 The Pauli Matrices (泡利矩阵)

- standard

  - computational basis
    $$
    \begin{equation}
    X\equiv\begin{bmatrix}0&1\\1&0\end{bmatrix},Z\equiv\begin{bmatrix}1&0\\0&-1\end{bmatrix}
    \end{equation}
    $$

  - identity operator $I$
    $$

    $$

- Pauli matrices are all Hermitian, unitary, they square to the identity, and their eigenvalues are $\pm1$

- Pauli matrices either commute or anticommute

#### 3.3.5 Rotation Operators

    $$

#### 3.4.1 Probability, expectation, and variance of an operator

- Z operator
  - $Z=|0\rang \lang0|-|1\rang\lang1|$
  - Hermitian operator
    - $\prod_0\equiv|0\rang\lang0|$
    - $\prod_1\equiv|1\rang\lang1|$
    - projection operator because $\prod^2=\prod$
    - $Z=\prod_0-\prod_1$
- *von Neumann measurement*: for any orthonormal basis $\{|\phi_i\rang\}_{i\in\{0,1\}}$, the measurement operators are $\{|\phi_i\rang\lang\phi_i|\}_{i\in\{0,1\}}$, and the state reduces to $|\phi_i\rang\lang\phi_i|\psi\rang/|\lang\phi_i|\psi\rang|$ with probability $\lang\psi|\phi_i\rang\lang\phi_i|\psi\rang=|\lang\phi_i|\psi\rang|^2$.
- expectation
  - $\lang Z \rang\equiv\lang\psi|Z|\psi\rang$
- deviation
  - $\triangle Z\equiv\lang(Z-\lang Z\rang )^2\rang^{1/2}$
  - the uncertainty of the observable Z when the state is $|\psi\rang$
- variance
  - $(\triangle Z)^2$

#### 3.4.2 The uncertainty principle

- $$
  \triangle Z\triangle X\ge\frac{1}{2}|\lang\psi|[Z,X]|\psi\rang|
  $$

- define $Z_0\equiv Z-\lang Z\rang$ , $X_0\equiv X-\lang X\rang$
  $$
  \triangle Z\triangle X=\lang\psi|Z_0^2|\psi\rang^{1/2}\lang\psi|X_0^2|\psi\rang^{1/2}\ge|\lang\psi|Z_0X_0|\psi\rang|
  $$

- $Re\{A\}\equiv(A+A^T)/2$ , $Im\{A\}\equiv(A-A^T)/2i$
  $$
  \begin{equation}
  Re\{Z_0X_0\}=\frac{Z_0X_0+X_0Z_0}{2}\equiv \frac{\{Z_0,X_0\}}{2}
  \end{equation}
  $$

  $$
  \begin{equation}
  Im\{Z_0X_0\}=\frac{Z_0X_0-X_0Z_0}{2i}\equiv \frac{[Z_0,X_0]}{2i}
  \end{equation}
  $$

- $$
  \begin{align}
  |\lang\psi|Z_0X_0|\psi\rang|&=|\lang\psi|Re\{Z_0X_0\}|\psi\rang+i\lang\psi|Im\{Z_0X_0\}|\psi\rang|\\
  &\ge|\lang\psi|Im\{Z_0X_0\}|\psi\rang|\\
  &=|\lang\psi|[Z_0,X_0]|\psi\rang|/2\\
  &=|\lang\psi|[Z,X]|\psi\rang|/2
  \end{align}
  $$

#### 3.5.4 The No-Cloning theorem (量子态不可克隆原理) 

- impossible to build a *universal copier* of quantum states

  - a universal copier would be a device that could copy any arbitrary quantum state that is input to it

- proof

  - suppose operator $U$ acting as a universal copier

  - input $|\psi\rang=\alpha |0\rang+\beta|1\rang$ as the first qubit and input an ancilla qubit $|0\rang$ as the second qubit, such a device should "write" the first qubit to the second qubit slot as follows:
    $$
    \begin{align}
    U|\psi\rang|0\rang&=|\psi\rang|\psi\rang\\
    &=(\alpha|0\rang+\beta|1\rang)(\alpha|0\rang+\beta|1\rang)\\
    &=\alpha^2|0\rang|0\rang+\alpha\beta|0\rang|1\rang+\alpha\beta|1\rang|0\rang+\beta^2|1\rang|1\rang
    \end{align}
    $$

  - in particular, it also copies the states $|0\rang$ and $|1\rang$ as follows:
    $$
    U|0\rang|0\rang=|0\rang|0\rang,U|1\rang|0\rang=|1\rang|1\rang
    $$

  - imply on a superposition $\alpha|0\rang+\beta|1\rang$ as follows:
    $$
    U(\alpha|0\rang+\beta|1\rang)|0\rang=\alpha|0\rang|0\rang+\beta|1\rang|1\rang
    $$

  - contradiction
    $$
    \exists\alpha,\beta:\alpha^2|0\rang|0\rang+\alpha\beta|0\rang|1\rang+\alpha\beta|1\rang|0\rang+\beta^2|1\rang|1\rang\neq\alpha|0\rang|0\rang+\beta|1\rang|1\rang
    $$

- another proof by exploiting unitarity of quantum evolutions

  - suppose operator $U$ acting as a universal copier

  - consider 2 arbitrary states $|\psi\rang$ and $|\phi\rang$
    $$
    U|\psi\rang|0\rang=|\psi\rang|\psi\rang,U|\phi\rang|0\rang=|\phi\rang|\phi\rang
    $$

  - consider the probability amplitude $\lang\psi|\lang\psi||\phi\rang|\phi\rang$
    $$
    \lang\psi|\lang\psi||\phi\rang|\phi\rang=\lang\psi|\phi\rang\lang\psi|\phi\rang=\lang\psi|\phi\rang^2
    $$

  - using the property $U^TU=I$ :
    $$
    \begin{align}
    \lang\psi|\lang\psi||\phi\rang|\phi\rang&=\lang\psi|\lang0|U^TU|\phi\rang|0\rang\\
    &=\lang\psi|\lang0||\phi\rang|0\rang\\
    &=\lang\psi|\phi\rang\lang0|0\rang\\
    &=\lang\psi|\phi\rang
    \end{align}
    $$

  - consequence:
    $$
    \lang\psi|\lang\psi||\phi\rang|\phi\rang=\lang\psi|\phi\rang^2=\lang\psi|\phi\rang
    $$
    only for $\lang\psi|\phi\rang=1$ and $\lang\psi|\phi\rang=0$ , means the two states are orthogonal to each other

- several applications

  - the security of the quantum key distribution protocol
  - application in quantum Shannon theory because we can use it to reason about the quantum capacity of a certain quantum channel known as the erasure channel.

### 3.7 Summary and Extensions to Qudit States

- qudit: on d-dimensional systems

#### 3.7.1 Qudits

- a qudit state $|\psi\rang$ is an arbitrary superposition of some set of orthonormal basis states $\{|j\rang\}_{j\in\{0,...,d-1\}}$ for a d-dimensional quantum syste
  $$
  |\psi\rang\equiv\sum_{j=0}^{d-1}\alpha_j|j\rang
  $$

- the amplitudes $\alpha_j$ obey the normalization condition $\sum_{j=0}^{d-1}|\alpha_j|^2=1$

#### 3.7.2 Unitary evolution

- cyclic shift operator $X(x)$:

  - $$
    X(x)|j\rang=|x\oplus j\rang
    $$

  - $\oplus$ is a cyclic addition operator, result is $(x+j) mod (d)$

  - $X(x)$ is a qudit generalization of the $X$ Pauli operator

#### 3.7.3 Measurement of qudits

- Hermitian operator $A$

  - $$
    A=\sum_jf(j)\Pi_j
    $$

  - $\Pi_j\Pi_k=\Pi_j\delta_{j,k}$ and $\sum_j\Pi_j=I$

  - A measurement of the operator $A$ then returns the result $j$ with the following probability
    $$
    p(j)=\lang\psi|\Pi_j|\psi\rang
    $$

  - resulting state
    $$
    \frac{\Pi_j|\psi\rang}{\sqrt{p(j)}}
    $$

  - expectation of operator $A$
    $$
    \mathbb{E}(A)=\sum_jf(j)\lang\psi|\Pi_j|\psi\rang=\lang\psi|\sum_jf(j)\Pi_j|\psi\rang=\lang\psi|A|\psi\rang
    $$
